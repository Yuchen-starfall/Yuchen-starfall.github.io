The classical hypothesis of heterosis

  • Dominance hypothesis (dominant complementation hypothesis) – 同位点聚合

    There are deleterious or unfavorable allelic variations at different loci in inbred lines, manifesting as recessive (隐性) alleles. Hybridization combines multiple dominant alleles from both parental lines in hybrids and effectively conceals the phenotypic impact of recessive alleles. （显性对隐性的掩盖）

  • Overdominance hypothesis – 不同位点聚合

    Different alleles do not exhibit superiority or inferiority, or different alleles exhibit superiority at different growth stages. （杂合状态本身的优势）

  • Epistasis hypothesis

    In the contextof quantitative traits, where multiple genes collectively regulate the target traits, this hypothesis postulates that the effect of a particular gene on the target traits can be influenced by one or more other genes.

同时，三个假说中的任意一个并不能解释杂种优势现象，很多时候是共同作用的结果。

Additive and dominant QTLs (quantitative trait loci) predominate in vegetative growth stage, while epistatic QTLs are activated and become dominant as plants enter the floral transition period(Xiao et al. 2021). Both dominant and overdominant QTLs play a role in yield heterosis.

Genetic features of crop heterosis

  • Trait specificity

    遗传优势的大小（株高易出现、品质不易出现）、组成（The genetic effects of allelic interactions）存在性状特异性

  • Quantifiability

    Dosage-sensitive （剂量敏感形）

    East(1936) : “the problem of heterosis is the problem of the inheritance of quantitative characters.”

  • Context dependence

    (Huang et al. 2016)展示出了杂种优势的大小主要由累积的杂种优势位点（显性互补）或杂合的杂种优势位点（超显性）数量决定，而不是由全基因组杂合性决定。两系法和三系法育种系统的水稻杂种分别利用了不同的杂种优势位点。杂种优势位点的遗传效应依赖于遗传背景。

  • Gene balance hypothesis

    Heterosis is largely affected by the kinetics and assembly modes of multisubunit protein complexes, which include various regulatory components functioning in hierarchical networks.

    In the dominance hypothesis, the deleterious recessive alleles encode the unstable or inefficient proteins, and crossing rapidly aggregates alleles encoding the stable protein from both parents and downregulates unstable alleles; in the overdominance hypothesis, 2 different alleles from both parents are stable, and they function in different tissues, growth periods or environmental conditions, and crossing brings them together and provides hybrid higher activity and stability of the protein over multiple tissues, growth stages, and conditions.

    The model elucidates that homozygous function constraints in an insufficient background are the core molecular mechanism underlying heterosis.
